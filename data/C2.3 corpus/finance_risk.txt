Risk management in algorithmic trading requires agents with strict permission boundaries. A trading agent should be allowed to read market data and portfolio state but must require explicit human approval before placing buy or sell orders. Stop-loss triggers are high-stakes actions: executing them incorrectly can lock in large losses. Agents must maintain a full decision log for regulatory compliance. Diversification across uncorrelated asset classes reduces systemic risk; an agent can compute correlation matrices to surface over-concentration before proposing trades.
